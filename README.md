## What Is Filepass, Exactly?

The pitch is simple: one link, one place, no more mess.

You upload your mix, send your client a link, and they can stream it right in their browser — no account needed on their end, no app download, no password to remember. They listen, they drop time-stamped comments ("the snare feels a bit loud at 1:32"), and those comments turn into a checklist on your side that you can tick off as you work through them.

That paywall feature is genuinely clever: you can lock the download behind payment, so the client literally cannot download the final files until they've paid you. The money clears, the download unlocks. No more "I'll get you the rest next week" conversations.

Files never expire either. Unlike WeTransfer links that vanish after a week, your projects stay live until you decide to delete them.

## The Features That Actually Matter

**Time-stamped comments** — Clients click a spot on the waveform and type their note. No more "the part around the middle of the song where the guitars come in." You know exactly where they mean.

**Revision checklist** — Every comment auto-converts to a to-do item. Check it off when it's done. If you have five active clients at once, this alone saves you from losing your mind.

**Client uploads** — Clients can drag-and-drop session files, stems, or reference tracks directly into their project folder. No login required. Works for huge files too.

**Lossless streaming** — Filepass does zero encoding. What you upload is what the client hears. No 128kbps MP3 artifacts sneaking into your feedback sessions.

**Custom branding** — Swap out the Filepass logo for your own. Looks more professional, clients trust it more, and nobody needs to know what platform you're using behind the scenes. (Pro plan only.)

**Embeddable Portfolio Player** — Build a lossless listening portfolio and drop it on your website. For engineers trying to land new clients, this is a genuinely nice-to-have. (Pro plan only.)

It's a strong fit if you're:

- A **mixing or mastering engineer** doing client work and drowning in revision emails
